Designing an enterprise product that requires little to no training?


No problem


Designing the tool that controls how it scales to the needs of hundreds of multinational companies across multiple business verticals?


Hmmm


Lack of clarity + research = interesting secrets


Our product had a number of sizable clients who were scheduled to onboard onto our platform, and we had a team of support people ready to work. What was needed was a tool to help our support team manage the large volume of organizational complexities that would surely accompany the above clients.


The only thing that was handed to us was an unused legacy product that was our sole source of concrete details and functional specification. The name: ADM.


What we didn’t have was clear understanding of the exact details of how ADM *should* facilitate all of those things. Picking it apart a bit. I inventoried the entirety of ADM. It turns out that there were over 300 different fields and controls, distributed across over 60 screens/sections.


Hmm


The first step had to be understanding what ADM is from the stakeholder and the subject matter expert; in this case my team lead was both. We followed that up with interviewing our support team in Boulder, remotely. Their support experience was actually through an entirely different product. The name: GLO.


We now have two products, ADM and GLO, and there was no single unified vision for what the product should be, while jogging alongside was history of another product with an entirely different client base.


Back to the support team. One of the most important insights we learned from them was this: The entire universe of their work is really focused around only two things: setting up organizations and allocating the people within. The remaining abstractions were only served to support these two purposes.


300 fields and 60 screens, that was...


As we were rushing to establish the early designs, a conversation with one of our lead devs revealed something...both good and problematic, about the relationship between ADM and GLO...


To see what we learned, our experiments in information architecture, lo-fi prototyping, designing in hi-fi alongside developers under pressure, and how all of it led to an unlikely successful launch (and the warts that came along with it), send me an email and say hi.
